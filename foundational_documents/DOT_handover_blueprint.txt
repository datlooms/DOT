===========================================================================
 1. WHY THIS PROJECT EXISTS
===========================================================================

The operator (Anthony) trades US30 on a FTMO $100K prop-firm account. The goal
is a fully automated signal engine that identifies high-probability directional
setups and enters trades with no screen time, while the EA's proven dynamic
stop management handles exits — and that survives FTMO's hard risk gates
(−$2,500 daily loss, −$10,000 total drawdown) with wide margin.

The solution is equiDOT: a portfolio of convergence signals that fire only when
several market variables reach distributional extremes simultaneously (or in a
short lagged sequence) in the same direction, while the D2D SuperTrend confirms
the bias. When multiple independent measures all agree at the same moment AND
the trend framework confirms, the probability of a clean directional move is
high. The system is survival-first: the daily-loss ceiling is honored before
any profitability metric.

 2. THE FULL RESEARCH JOURNEY (how BOOK-50 was reached)
===========================================================================

This is the honest history, including the wrong turns, because the wrong turns
are where the method was forged.

  (a) THE 766K DISCOVERY ON BROKEN DATA.
      An early full-scope convergence discovery ran ~766K candidate signals on
      a dataset that turned out to be corrupt — a state-column shift and a
      cold-start feature break. The discovery still "worked" in the sense that
      it surfaced structure, but the numbers were unusable. The failure was a
      map, not a verdict: it exposed the data break, a feature-overlap problem,
      and the need for new variables. The dataset was rebuilt from source
      (core.py), Auditor-ratified, parity 88/88 at 6 decimals.

  (b) THE 100x UNITS BUG.
      Every dollar figure in the early era was inflated 100×: USD_PER_POINT_
      PER_LOT was set to 100.0 when FTMO US30.cash is $1/point/1.0 lot. This
      distorted worst-days and P&L across the board and caused good systems to
      be wrongly dismissed on apparently catastrophic worst-days. Fixing it to
      1.0 superseded all prior figures. LESSON: distortion in the accounting
      layer poisons every downstream judgment. Every number in BOOK-50 is at
      true $1/pt.

  (c) TIC-PROOF RE-ANALYSIS.
      After the fixes, the field was re-scored through the ratified engine on a
      sealed baseline (152,983 rows, Jan 19 – Jun 25 2026, 6,900-bar warmup),
      with every per-signal number traceable to source (signal_full_records.csv)
      and every per-day P&L to signal_per_day_pnl.jsonl. No re-derivation, no
      proxies — the engine of record is the arbiter.

  (d) THE DIAMOND, AND THE BOOK LINEAGE.
      diamond-35   — the loss-decorrelated F0 core (35 triple-convergence
                     signals selected on independent losing days, OOS-clean).
      -> BOOK-40   — + 5 within-vocabulary co-firers. These RAISED OOS PF
                     (5.54 -> 6.54) and improved worst-day — a clean add.
      -> BOOK-58   — + 18 structure-balanced persistent fillers. These raised
                     net and, critically, BEAR net (the March crash), but
                     diluted OOS PF. Real value, wrong-density.
      -> BOOK-48   — leave-one-out trim of the 10 worst book-level eroders
                     (bear-net contributors protected). This RECOVERED per-trade
                     quality: OOS PF back to 6.65, the highest on record, while
                     keeping 85% of BOOK-58's net and 81% of its bear net.
                     BOOK-48 is the F0 OOS-PF ceiling.
      -> BOOK-50   — + 2 F1 sequential structure-fills. These close the only two
                     market structures F0 could not decorrelate into the book
                     (SQUEEZE_BREAKOUT, TREND_EXHAUSTION), at worst-day held
                     (−$127.5) and a small OOS-PF give-back (6.65 -> 6.54).
                     BOOK-50 is the structure-complete final engine of record.

  (e) THREE FAMILIES TESTED AND JUDGED ON EVIDENCE.
      +18 structure fillers — reopened after a crude dismissal; found to carry
        the bear. RECOVERED (they seeded BOOK-58 -> trimmed into BOOK-48).
      concurrence DEPTH — reopened cleanly and engine-scored; pure depth WR ~76%
        / PF ~1.1, and depth-without-named-triples PF < 1 (net-losing). GENUINE
        NULL. Correctly excluded. (The original ~89% was a units-distortion/
        small-sample artifact.)
      F1 SEQUENTIAL family — a different (lagged) trigger. Tested with the same
        discipline: real and persistent but a weaker parallel engine; redundant
        for NET (more F0 does it cheaper and safer), but UNIQUELY able to fill
        the 2 missing structures. Two F1 signals earned a place for coverage.

  (f) THE CONCURRENCY INVESTIGATION (position-count cap -> live-risk jar).
      With the book frozen, the concurrency control itself was re-examined. Three
      findings, each decided by the engine, not intuition:
        DEPTH IS A REAL CAUSAL QUALITY GRADIENT. Recomputing each trade's entry-depth
          from ONLY positions open at-or-before its entry bar (strictly causal, known
          at entry), the loss rate falls monotonically — 8.6% at depth 1-3, 4.5% at
          4-7, 0.0% at depth 8+ — PF climbing 5.0 -> infinity. Deeper concurrence is a
          genuinely better trade, and it is knowable at the moment of entry.
        SIZING UP DEEP STACKS IS A CORRELATED-TAIL LANDMINE. Deep stacks are
          same-direction and clustered, so up-sizing them concentrates risk exactly
          when the most positions are open; one violent reversal mid-stack stops the
          cluster together. Aggressive depth-up schedules blew the drawdown ceiling
          15-24x under that correlated stress (−$10k to −$17k). Correctly rejected.
        THE BE-AWARE LIVE-RISK JAR IS THE RESOLUTION. The insight that reopened it: a
          position at break-even carries ZERO further risk (stop at entry), so it must
          neither block a new entry nor count toward the risk bound. The jar bounds
          LIVE (pre-BE) risk to 6 lots — each trade takes 1 lot, a break-even'd winner
          frees its lot, a new signal opens only with room. It counts RISK, not bodies.
          Validated: +74 trades, +$1,418 net, at the IDENTICAL 6-lot hard bound, same
          worst-day and max-DD — a strict improvement over the position-count cap.

  (g) THE EXECUTION-MODEL DECISION (per-tick -> per-bar SL management).
      The EA had been switched to manage the SL PER-TICK, intending to "lock in sooner"
      against single-minute whipsaws. Tracing it against the sim exposed the opposite effect:
      with LOCK_FRAC = 1.0 the BE lock lands on the tier-1/trigger price, so PER-TICK the SL
      arms directly onto current price and the very next adverse tick scratches the trade —
      runners collapse (387 -> 102) before they can develop. The sim (and the validated book)
      is PER-BAR: it repositions the SL only at bar boundaries off the closed bar's High/Low,
      which holds the armed stop through the minute's intrabar noise and lets trades reach
      tier 3+. Bracketed on the sealed baseline: per-bar $58,685 (the validated book) vs a
      per-tick worst-case ~$33.9k — live-per-tick can only diverge DOWNWARD, and where it
      lands depends on intrabar tick order (unresolvable from OHLC; a demo/tick run settles it).
      DECISION: revert to PER-BAR. The broker still holds a hard SL that executes intrabar on
      any tick (downside always live); only the EA's REPOSITIONING is per-bar. This is a parity
      fix — per-bar management is what makes live == the validated book.

  (h) THE LOSS-REDUCTION SEARCH (what finally worked: room, not restriction).
      A systematic hunt for a way to cut the ~194 SL losers. Everything that REMOVED or
      RESTRICTED failed: a price-action loser filter (scan of the whole micro family) found
      ZERO variables that cut losers without cutting more winners — the losers are statistically
      invisible at entry; tighter initial stops added losers; a two-step breakeven lock scratched
      ~950 developing winners to save ~106 losses (net -$21k). The ONE thing that worked was the
      opposite instinct — giving strong-signal entries MORE room: a momentum-conditional wider
      initial SL, min(ATR×4, 150) on momentum entries (lr×dir >= 0.00012), non-momentum unchanged
      at min(ATR×2, 150). Honest framing: since 54% pin to the cap, it is really "let momentum
      entries use up to the $150 ceiling you already set" (mean stop $86 -> $131) — the $150 cap
      is never exceeded. Validated: 194 -> 166 SL, WR 91.9% -> 92.8%, PF 5.83 -> 6.12, OOS PF 6.57
      -> 6.99, min-fold 5.01 -> 5.49, worst-day -127.5 and max-DD -165.6 HELD, runners untouched,
      net $58,249 (-$436). A QUALITY lift for a rounding-error of net. The lesson: you cannot
      filter or stop your way out of the losers (they look like winners at entry); you can only
      give the borderline ones room to recover. Room helps; taking room hurts.

  The recurring lesson across all three: a weak or negative result is a data
  point that redirects, not a verdict that closes a door. Depth stayed closed
  because the clean test said so; the +18 and F1 opened because the clean test
  said so. Same rigor, outcomes decided by evidence.

 4. THE ANTI-CURVE-FIT METHODOLOGY (why the edge is believed real)
===========================================================================

  1. TRUE-SCALE ACCOUNTING. Every figure is $1/point/1.0 lot. No inflated units.
  2. TIC-PROOF DATA. Per-signal numbers trace to signal_full_records.csv; per-day
     P&L to signal_per_day_pnl.jsonl. No re-derivation drift.
  3. LOSS-DECORRELATION SELECTION. Signals chosen for INDEPENDENT losing days
     (per-day loss vectors, greedy add keeping the worst-day highest), NOT for
     in-sample PF. This is the opposite objective from fitting and cannot be
     gamed by memorizing the sample. Naive union of all 254 = PF 1.89;
     decorrelated = PF 6+.
  4. PERSISTENCE AT THREE SCALES. 6/6 monthly folds AND ISO-week positivity AND
     all-weekday coverage — simultaneously. A ≥30-trade floor bars small-sample
     flukes.
  5. OUT-OF-SAMPLE GATE. Select on Jan–Apr, measure on unseen May–Jun. The core
     books hold or STRENGTHEN OOS (diamond 5.54, BOOK-40 6.54). Overfitting
     cannot strengthen OOS.
  6. LEAVE-ONE-OUT TRIM THAT FIGHTS FIT. The trim REMOVED signals with high
     standalone PF (up to 21.7) because their losses were book-correlated —
     optimizing against the seductive in-sample numbers.
  7. NULL-HONORING. Lines that failed their nulls were killed: the depth signal
     (circular-shift null failed), the flip-trajectory model (AUC 0.492, below
     chance). A process that reports its own nulls is not manufacturing signal.
  8. A REAL BEAR IN THE SAMPLE. The March −10% war crash is inside the data; the
     book stays green through it.
  9. NULL / SHUFFLE TEST. The finished book was tested against three shuffles that
     break the signal->outcome link while keeping the EXACT trade management and
     trade count, 200+ iterations each: direction-shuffle (randomize each trade's
     side), random-timing (enter on random eligible bars — the TM-alone test), and
     fully random. The real book landed BEYOND THE MAXIMUM of all three nulls
     (0/200), ~16σ above the direction-shuffle mean and ~35σ above the random means
     (p < 0.005 on all three). Critically, random-timing through the real TM LOST
     money (net −$678, PF 0.97) — proving the edge is the SIGNAL, not the trade
     management. With OOS strengthening on top, the result is decisive.

  THE HONEST RESIDUAL: six months is one sample. It contains a crash-and-recover
  but no grinding sustained bear, no prolonged sideways chop, no rate-shock. The
  edge is proven IN the sampled regimes and OOS-validated within them; regimes
  not sampled are unproven, not disproven. Execution parity (real spread beyond
  the modeled 3.0 pts, fills, 1-minute slippage) is a live question, not a
  backtest one. This is the normal boundary between a validated backtest and
  live capital — not a flaw in the work. The demo is the true final test.

 6. WHAT REMAINS (Stage 9 — the EA build)
===========================================================================

  The signal set and trade management are validated and frozen. The remaining
  work takes the CURRENT equiDOT.cs (frozen, compiles 0/0) to BOOK-50 through
  the full pipeline (Developer -> Supervisor -> Auditor -> human confirm +
  MetaEditor 0/0). SEVEN changes, no more:

    (a) MOMENTUM-RUNNER TM BRANCH. In the LeapFrog trail only, switch lag = 3
        when the entry bar's Micro_LogReturn × direction ≥ 0.00012, else 2.
        Everything else in S.7 is untouched.

    (b) SEQUENTIAL-LATCH SUBSYSTEM for the 2 F1 signals. Two small ring buffers
        (≤15 bars) latch (leg-A-extreme AND ST_Flip) for Sqz_Val and ADX_Rising;
        on each bar, a lagged lookup (buffer[bar − k]) AND-ed with the current
        leg-B condition fires the signal. Reuses existing FEAT computations and
        the already-tracked ST_Flip anchor. This is a bounded state add — NOT a
        second engine.

    (c) 50-SIGNAL SWAP. Replace the current signal set with the BOOK-50 panel
        (48 F0 triples + 2 F1 sequential), grouped by the 8 structures.

    (d) LIVE-RISK JAR. Replace the MAX_POSITIONS=6 count cap with a 6-lot live-risk
        jar: a running live-lot counter (+1 on open; −1 at break-even, when the
        winner's lot leaves the jar; admit a new signal only when live lots < 6).
        It counts live risk, not open positions — break-even'd winners no longer
        block new entries. Causal (the live-lot count is known at entry). A
        sacred/S.7 change; human authorization granted.

    (e) PER-BAR EXECUTION MODEL. Revert DOTS SL management from per-tick to per-bar:
        gate the reposition logic (ManageDotsPositions) to new-bar detection and compute
        arm / tier / trail off the CLOSED bar's High/Low (High[1]/Low[1]) — not the live Bid
        tick, not the Close. Per-bar sequence per open position: (1) exit-check vs current SL,
        (2) tiers off closed-bar high/low, (3) arm/lock BE, (4) trail — reproducing
        portfolio_simulation_engine.py. The broker's hard SL still executes intrabar on any
        tick (downside always live); only the EA's REPOSITIONING is per-bar. Parity fix —
        per-bar is the validated $58,685 book; per-tick diverges downward. Sacred/S.7 change;
        human authorization granted.

    (f) MOMENTUM-CONDITIONAL WIDER INITIAL SL. At the order-open path where initial_risk is
        set from ATR (SECTION 8.6, risk = MathMin(atr*Dots_SL_Mult, Dots_SL_Cap)), make the
        ATR multiple momentum-conditional: if v = Micro_LogReturn x dir >= 0.00012 use
        min(ATR×4, 150), else min(ATR×2, 150). Reuses the runner's OWN v (no new variable).
        The $150 cap (Dots_SL_Cap) is inviolate and RISK_MULT / Dots_SL_Mult = 2.0 stays the
        base (S.17 unaltered) — this is a momentum-conditional branch, same pattern as the
        runner's lag-2/lag-3. Quality lift: 194 -> 166 SL, WR -> 92.8%, OOS PF -> 6.99, worst-day
        held, net $58,249 (-$436). Sacred/S.7 change; human authorization granted.

    (g) CONVICTION SELF-SCALING + GAP-SINGLES (S.20, ADOPTED). The EA scales its OWN lot size by
        conviction and adds two gap-only single-variable entries, all sharing the 6-lot jar:
        (1) at a book-LONG entry, Micro_Hurst > adaptive p90 -> 2.0 lots else 1.0 (LONGS ONLY, no
        short edge); a book long within 5 bars of a Micro_FailedBreak-extreme -> 1.25 (both -> higher).
        (2) two gap-singles fire only when zero Dots positions are open: Micro_Hurst>p97 & D2D=+1
        LONG, and Micro_FailedBreak>p90 & D2D=-1 (counter) LONG; each 1 lot at LOCK=3, gate ADX>=15 &
        Vol>=300. (3) per-bar: book signal -> Hurst-size 2x/1x; else-if flat -> the 2 gap-singles;
        single blocked while any Dots live. DEPLOY 1-LOT BASE ONLY (the multiplier IS the scaling;
        blanket-2x on top breaches the FTMO gate at a 12-lot stack). Result: 2,691 tr, WR 92.2%, PF
        6.15, net $89,487, worst-day -153.7, OOS PF 6.81 (pack-engine honest reproduction; the earlier
        modelled $90,103 carried a research-harness jar-sharing artifact — 0.7% correction, no
        conclusion changes). RATIONALE: press where the edge is strongest

        deepen a stacked day), flat otherwise — net +54% while worst-day barely moves. S.7-class change;
        human-confirmed/ADOPTED, pending Stage-9 build + full pipeline audit.
    (h) D2D CROWN JEWEL (S.21, RATIFIED -- Auditor PASS 2026-07-17, BUILT). The founding D2D
        signal is now a THREE-role member: (1) directional gate [existing]; (2) D2D-CONVICTION
